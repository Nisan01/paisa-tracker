Got it! Let’s break each section down:

1. **Top Summary Section**

   * What: Display the user’s overall financial snapshot.
   * How: Pull the total balance by aggregating all accounts. Show total income (sum of all income transactions) and total expenses (sum of all expense transactions).
   * Why: Gives users the most basic, at-a-glance financial picture.

2. **Income vs Expense Trends**

   * What: A time-based chart showing income and expenses over days, weeks, or months.
   * How: Plot total income (sum by date) and total expenses (sum by date) on a line chart.
   * Why: Helps users see trends—when they spend more or when they earn more.

3. **Biggest Expense Categories**

   * What: Show the categories where most money is spent.
   * How: Use a pie chart that breaks down expenses by category (e.g., food, bills, etc.).
   * Why: Gives users a sense of which categories take up the most of their budget.

4. **Loan Overview**

   * What: A breakdown of all loans the user has (whether lending or borrowing).
   * How: A table that lists each loan: person’s name, total amount, remaining balance, due date, and status.
   * Why: Keeps the user on top of who owes them or who they owe, with easy tracking of due dates.

5. **Budget Progress**

   * What: Track how close users are to their monthly budget limits by category.
   * How: Progress bars that show the percentage of budget used per category. For example, if food is 70% used, it highlights that.
   * Why: Helps users stay within their planned budgets and avoid overspending.

6. **Notifications/Alerts**

   * What: Alerts for important reminders.
   * How: Small badges or icons that highlight upcoming loan due dates, budget limit warnings, etc.
   * Why: Keeps users informed about critical dates or budget risks.

7. **Account List**

   * What: A list of all user accounts (e.g., bank, cash, eSewa, etc.).
   * How: Cards or rows that show each account name and its current balance.
   * Why: Gives a clear breakdown of where funds sit, so users know how much they have in each account.

This should help you visualize each part and figure out what data or interactions you need per section!
